These are the links to the individual chapters of the free online book Introduction to Financial Markets and Instruments, which is used in the Economics 104 - Financial Economics course taught at Harvey Mudd College by Professor Gary Evans.

This book is offered in only this format (chapter by chapter). This book is constantly being revised and updated, chapter by chapter, throughout the year, typically one chapter at a time. There is seldom any chapter in this book that is more than one year old. As can be seen below, the date of the version of each chapter is listed and no two chapters have the same date.

In a field like finance, markets and instruments change so rapidly that any text that attempts to explain how these markets work must be frequently updated. This is best done by a dynamic online textbook - one that is going through a constant revision at all times.

The reader will note that the numerous examples in the chapters are very current. They may be drawing upon incidents that are only a few weeks old. Likewise the graphs and data are very current.

The chapters are printed in pdf format, which are easy to download and print, or simply read through a viewer or computer screen. Click on the title.

The Chapters

Chapter Title Revision Date
1 Introduction to the World of Stocks (Equities) August 14, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
This chapter is designed to introduce students to stocks and the stock market. It defines stocks, explains what you are buying when you buy them, the exchanges, the role played by brokers and other intermediaries, market indexes like the S&P500, stock listings and IPOs, ADRs, and introduces mutual funds and exchange traded products (ETPs or ETFs).
2 Types of Stock Orders and Order Routing August 29, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
How you submit an order to buy or sell a stock, how the order is listed, the difference between market orders and limit orders, Best Bid and Best Ask and how these are disseminated, Level II and DOM quotations, short sales, the role of market makers and high-speed traders, large order processing and Dark Markets.
3 A Supply and Demand Model for Stocks September 2, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
This chapter introduces an elementary supply and demand model for stocks to explain theoretically why the price of a stock will rise or fall. It emphasizes the variables that move the market or individual stocks and introduces four-quadrant analysis. [This is not a topical chapter so is not revised as frequently as the others].
4 Historical Stock Market Performance September 19, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
This chapter discusses general stock market performance at an introductory level, using history as a guide. Topics include the role played by corporate earnings and projected earnings, price-to-earnings benchmarks, interest rates and portfolio effects, sales growth, dividends, mergers and acquisitions, speculation and momentum.
5 Mutual Funds September 24, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
This chapter provides an introduction to mutual funds, including a discussion of their role in retirement accounts, how they are structured, tax features, fee structure, and how to research them and shop for them, and how to buy them online. This chapter explains different mutual fund categories and some of the benefits and disadvantages of mutual funds.
6 Exchange Traded Products (ETPs and ETFs) October 12, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
This chapter discusses one of the fastest growing and most popular areas of investment, exchange traded products (also referred to as exchange traded funds). Similar in many respects to mutual funds, but overcoming many of the disadvantages of mutual funds, ETPs are now required knowledge for even the most basic retail investment account. But in addition to the advantages over mutual funds, many ETPs are for more risky than mutual funds and some should be avoided altogether by the retail investor. This chapter explains the differences between ETPs and mutual funds.
7 The Market for Yield-Bearing Financial Assets: Bonds, Notes and Bills October 21, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
A descripton and discussion of the market for U.S. Treasury bills, notes, and bonds, corporate and muncipal bonds, and other yield-bearing financial assets. This chapter includes an extensive discusion of risk and ratings for risk, and the impact of risk, maturities, and interest rate movements upon bond and note values. Bond mutual funds and ETFs are described and their risk profiles explored. There is considerable discussion of the role that yield-bearing assets should play in any investment portfolio.
8 Bond and Note Valuation and Related Interest Rate Formulas October 22, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
This is a highly technical chapter that mathematically describes the relation between bond and note prices and yields in the secondary market for these securities. This chapter requires an advanced knowledge of algebra to be understood. Unfortunately, bond pricing cannot be understood without knowing the underlying mathematics. This chapter can be skipped by any reader lacking the required math background.
9 Put and Call Options October 31, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
This is the original chapter for this book, first written in 1995. This chapter defines and explains put and call options, describes their purpose and their markets and how they are bought and sold online. There is a discussion of introductory price theory along with a description of elementary options trading strategies.
10 Futures Contracts November 15, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
This chapter offers an introduction to futures contracts and how they are designed and traded., including a description of contract specifications, settlement and delivery conditions, and margin requirements. There is an introduction to futures trading strategies, how futures are used for hedging, and the role played by futures contracts in certain types of ETPs (especially exchange traded notes, or ETNs). Special attention is given to crude oil and natural gas futures.
11 Residential Real Estate as an Investment and a Place to Live November 22, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
As the title suggests, this chapter treats the home in which you live from the perspective of a financial investment.(There is no discussion of commercial real estate investments in this book). A modeled investment perspective is offered, along with some general tips about finding and buying a home. The chapter has a lengthy discussion of mortgage options and how they differ, along with a brief discussion of the warnings implied by the mortgage meltdown in 2008 and all of the lessons offered by that experience. There is also a discussion of the tax advantages of home real estate.
AppA Retirement Accounts September 27, 2019

[Ready for use in Fall 2019 E104]
This is a short and cryptic chapter about different kinds of formal retirement accounts like 401-K, Roth IRA and other IRA accounts, and describes their features including tax advantages and disadvantages, contribution limits and how they are used. This material is kept current for the present tax year (although it is not normally updated until after April 15 of any tax year). It is treated here as an appendix rather than a full chapter because it is linked to a lecture video that should be viewed any time this material is read. In Economics 104, the video is used instead of a classroom lecture.
