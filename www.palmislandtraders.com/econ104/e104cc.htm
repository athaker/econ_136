Reading and Homework Assignments - Fall 2019

The primary reading material used in this class is a free online book written by your teacher named Introduction to Financial Markets and Instruments. Because this online book is revised and updated throughout the semester (the reason is explained in the introduction to the book) you should read the material only when it is assigned, which will normally be just before the module begins (see below), or after the online chapter has the notation "[Ready for use in Fall 2019]."

In this class I lecture and assign reading, videos and homework assignments by modules, rather than week-by-week. This gives you some leeway in developing a reading schedule. It is very important that you try to keep up with the reading - background material is very important in this class.

All of the reading material and other assignments are located on the Course Content Page, which also includes all of the lecture slides used in this course plus video recordings of the lectures. The lecture slides are posted just before the lectures are given (so that they can be referred to during the lecture) and the videos are posted within 24 hours (typically) after the lecture is given.

There are not very many homework assignments in Economics 104. Homework assignments are typically not collected nor graded, but we will be discussing them on the dates that they are due. Additionally, keep all copies of these homework assignments. You may be required to use them on an examination.

Module 1 - Introduction to Equities

September 4 (Wed) to September 11 (Wed) : 3 sessions

Introduction to class. Discussion of general investment objectives, strategies, and tactics. NYSE, NASDAQ stocks, reading the quotes, indices, exchanges.

Read: Chapter 1 Introduction to the World of Stocks and Chapter 2 Types of Stock Orders and Order Routing

Watch this video about Level2 viewer and chart, AAPL, Sept. 10, 2013.

Module 2 - Equity Performance

September 16 (Mon) to September 25 (Wed) : 4 sessions

History of the stock market, leverage, classes of stock, electronic trading, some stock selection and trading strategies

Read: Chapter 3 A Supply and Demand Model for Stocks and Chapter 4 Historical Stock Market Performance

Watch this video about The Beta and the Sharpe Ratio, estimating risk and performance.

Do Stock Portfolio Assignment 1 (short and easy) after reading Chapter 4 and then between Friday, September 20, 1:30 PM and Monday, September 23, 6:00 AM (i.e. when the markets are closed), complete Stock Portfolio Assignment 2, print it, and bring it to class on Monday, September 23, or put in in my inbox outside of Parsons 1261. (No email accepted).

Module 3 - Mutual Funds and Retirement Accounts

September 30 (Mon) to October 2 (Wed) : 2 sessions

Mutual funds and classifications, fees, performance issues, index funds, portfolio strategies, quotations, online management.

The section on retirement accounts is covered only with reading and video (no lecture) and includes a discussion of the primary account types (Traditional IRA, Roth IRA, 401-k, 403-b etc.), rules that govern their use, contribution limitations and tax benefits.

Read: Appendix A Retirement Accounts , and watch a video Retirement Accounts (meant to accompany the Retirement Accounts Appendix).

Read: Chapter 5 Mutual Funds

First Examination - Monday, October 7

Module 4 - Exchange Traded Products

October 9 (Wed) to October 14 (Mon) : 2 sessions

Exchange Traded Products, including Exchange Traded Funds and Exchange Traded Notes, how they differ from mutual funds, benefits and disadvantages, classification based upon collateral, and their role in retirement accounts.

Read: Chapter 6 Exchange Traded Products

Module 5 - Yield-Bearing Financial Assets: Bonds, Notes and Bills

October 16 (Wed) to October 28 (Mon) : 3 sessions (Fall break is during this session)

U.S. Treasury markets, reading the quotes, bond values and interest rates, indexing formulas, bond mutual funds, corporate YBFAs, mortgage-backed markets, money market FAs, money market funds. CDOs, and the credit meltdown of 2007/2008, the remedies since 2009 (some of this covered in the real estate module).

Read: Chapter 7 The Market for Yield-Bearing Financial Assets: Bonds, Notes, and Bills and Chapter 8 Bond and Note Valuation and Related Interest Rate Formulas.

Module 6 - Put and Call Options

October 30 (Wed) to Nov 6 (Wed) : 3 sessions

Put and Call Options, trading strategies, writing covered calls, Aruba Options model.

Read: Chapter 9: Put and Call Options.

Second Examination - Monday, November 11

Module 7 - Futures Contracts

November 13 (Wed) to November 20 (Mon) : 3 sessions

Futures contracts and related derivatives, hedging strategies, speculation with futures and arbitrage with futures and other financial assets.

Read: Chapter 10: Futures Contracts

Module 8 - Real Estate and Investment Strategy

November 25 (Mon) to December 9 (Mon) : 4 sessions (Thanksgiving is during this period)

Real estate financing, market theory, leverage and dangers of leverage, connection to mortgage pass-thru market, home selection, tax issues, escrow, fees, housing, plus a general discussion of mid-term to long-term investment strategies, portfolios, diversification, mixed investments, investment goals, avoiding disasters.

Read: Chapter 11: Residential Real Estate as an Investment and a Place to Live

In-class portion of Final Examination - Wednesday, December 11th at class time and take-home final distributed on the same day.
Take-home final due Wednesday, December 18 noon at Parsons 1261 (sorry, no email submissions).
