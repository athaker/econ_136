Project: Mangatopia

Team: Teo Asinari, Ki Wan Gkoo, Sam Pramodh

Description: An app designed to resolve a long standing problem for non-japanese manga fans. We propose a manga reading service across devices which uses a novel pricing scheme and publisher contracts to provide fans with fast, cheap, and legal access to the content they love.

 

Project: TheCollegeSquare

Team: Avi Thaker, Brett Collins, Matt Cook

Description: A website/app that serves as a marketplace for students to list items that they would like to buy and sell, a college square or commons idea. We suggest a service that would be free to students to trade items, but charge local businesses to advertise goods and services on it.

 

Projected: Legisno

Team: Alex Melville and Sarah Stevens

Description: Legisno is a smartphone application that connects voters with the issues and politicians that impact their lives. Users can track a politician’s actions, view the vote count on legislation, and contact their leaders through the app.

 

Project: SecureShare

Team: Eric Storm & Michael Saffron

Description: An enterprise grade software suite that allows companies to collaborate in real-time on documents hosted on internal servers. By storing the information locally, companies can maintain full control over the security of their information.

 

Project: HouseIT

Team: Annie Chung, Sachit Sood and Mengyi Tao

Description: A website/app for college students to find short term or long term real estate properties to move off campus efficiently.

 

Project: HydroRing

Team: Ksenia Zakirova & Crystal Hsu

Description: A personal hydration monitoring system in the form of a color changing ring. The ring changes color when perspiration levels suggest that the wearer may be dehydrated, acting as a visible reminder to drink more water.

 

Project: MIBS

Team: Alejandro Alderman, Gourav Khadge, Jackie Ong, and Andrew Gibiansky

Description: An innovation in biotech based on 21st century design principles like open APIs and modular (customizable and replaceable) components.

 

Project: CareConnect Global

Team: Johannes Einarsson, Jirka Hladis, Demetri Monovoukas, Henry Pellicoro

Description: Care Connect Global is a revolutionary healthcare platform, connecting American patients with quality providers overseas.

 

Project: AdCon

Team: Nick Bougopoulos, Sam Kaplan, Liza Rizzo

Description: Mobile app that allows Twitter and Instagram users to re-tweet and re-post product advertisements from businesses for immediate cash through a mobile marketplace.

 

Project: Hopp

Team: Kenny Rabin and Sam Fox

Description: Hopp is a social mapping mobile app that allows bar, restaurant, and club goers to track hot and cold neighborhoods on any given night. The map is designed for big cities and perfect for residents looking for new experiences and visitors trying to figure how nightlife in major cities works by ensuring a user will never have a bad night out.

 

Project: My Space

Team: Kanna Jeyaseelan, Varun Kanwar, Rojesh Koirala, and Vanessa Ronan

Description: My Space is “StackExchange for Interior Design.”  It’s a platform for people to give and get personalized interior design advice through a sleek photo-friendly interface.  It connects homeowners with interior design enthusiasts so they can get free or cheap suggestions, while letting designers build a reputation, make money, and enjoy solving problems creatively.

 

Project: Conceptualive

Team: Caroline Ebinger, Hernando Flores, Christie Lee, Jesse Medina

Description: Our idea is to create a Facebook game to help people learn about the human body - to equip the world to keep themselves happy and healthy, without needing to hire trainers, nutritionists or dietitians. It's true, most people don't care to learn this stuff - it's no fun. We believe our game will be more than fun, we intend to make it addicting as hell. Our offer of value resides in the opportunity for gamers to gain coupons and discounts for a range of products and services - athletic apparel, fitness equipment, healthy restaurants, food products and even grocery stores as they advance through our gamified curriculum.
