Major Assignments and Support Material

(Don't use this until assigned - much of this will be refined or changed as this semester proceeds):

  • End-of-semester task: Writing a summary and reaction to Blitzscaling.

    • This assignment is required if you are not on a pitch team.

    Read Blitzscaling and either (a) write a short paper, about 500 words in length, that summarizes the primary theme in the book, then offer your reaction to book, or (b) create a reaction video doing the same and put it on YouTube (you can remove it later after I have watched it if you want).

  • About the HMC INQ and HMC INQ companies:

    • Go to the incubator website: HMC INQ to see what is happening at the incubator!

    Sergey Tsalkov is interviewed by Indie Hackers about ArkServers.io and building the company from scratch.

  • The Sam Altman video series How to Start a Startup

    • This is a Creative Commons video lecture series of 20 lectures arranged by Sam Altman (President of Y Combinator) and taught at Stanford in 2014 entitled How to Start a Startup I will assign two of these for discussion per week (and I will allow a little sloppiness - it is like having a separate course within a course). In the DA section above, the assignment for discussion will be labeled SAL1 (for lecture 1). No prepared questions are arranged for this series.

  • The NPR Guy Raz entreprenerial podcast series How I Built This

    • National Public Radio offers a podcast called How I Built This. These podcasts were first recommended by Kimberly Joly '18 and among the current selections she recommends:

    1. Spanx, Kate Spade, and Rent the Runway: big focus on the persistence and dedication the founders maintained to build their business
    2. Instagram: good example of how Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger pivoted from their initial idea to keep their business viable
    3. Airbnb: some clever ideas to raise money and how they convinced reluctant people that renting out their home is a good idea
    4. TRX: really goes through building his business step-by-step in detail, from developing his idea in a business class, to manufacturing the product himself, to fighting against counterfeit products.

    • Go to this Google Drive Doc called ss147_vid_s19 and add your embedded YouTube link as per instructions on the page. Just copy what is there now and sub in your info.

    Early in the course you are required to develop a product idea and pitch it to your peers by making a video. The instructions for doing that are found here: Product Proposal Pitch Instructions

    Finally, print and review the e&e Peer Consulting Guidelines for Courtesy.

  • Corporate Financial Design (Capitalization)

    • This is a complicated homework that is designed to show you how to design the capital structure of your startup given your strategic plans for fund raising and an exit. This homework requires you to (1) download an Excel zip file labeled Corporate Structure Design, then (2) download a homework assignment entitled Omsys , then (3) listen to a Video file labeled Capitalization Design. [Note: You need to listen to the video file, but if you want a copy of the slide set on which that file is based, here it is].

    You should download the Excel file first, open it and look at it, briefly read the homework assignment, then watch the Capitalization Design video. After watching the video, do the homework. Keep in mind that the Excel Corporate Structure Design workbook will also be used in class after you have formed your imaginary companies in order to design your cap plans for them.

  • Y-Combinator Pitch Guide

    • HMC INQ is modeled in part after Y-Combinator. Y-Combinator accepts new teams based upon video pitches, then each team gives a live pitch during "demo days." Here is the Guide to Demo Day Presentations that might help prepare for pitches in general.
